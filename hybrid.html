AAN IMPROVED HIGH QUALITY, REAL-TIME, HYBRID TEXT-TO-SPEECH SYNTHESIS SYSTEM

Authors:Feng-Long Xie, Xin-Hui Li, Wen-chao Su, Li Lu, Frank K. Soong (Submitted to ICASSP 2021)
Abstract: A trajectory tiling based hybrid text-to-speech system is revisted here to further enhance its synthesis performance. A combination of self-attention based encode and RNN based decoder architecture incorporating two-level (word level and Chinese phonetic alphabet letter level) linguistic representation is used to generate smooth speech parameter trajectory, and then the mel-spectrl distortion and RMSE of F0 between the generated trajectory and target candidates are used to construct a lattice. Finally Normalized cross-correlation is used to search for the best sequence of waveform “tiles” in the lattce. The subjective A/B preference experimental reuslts show that our proposed hybrid system WESYNTHESIS can outperform both the previous trajectory tiling hybrid system baseline with preference (56\% vs. 27\%) and the state-of-the-art real-time speech synthesis system which uses Tacotron 2 and LPCNet vocoder with prefercence (xx\% vs. xx\%).
